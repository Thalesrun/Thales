v0.3 is working. 12 bars settled, 92% win rate, +$23.45.

The shift from v0.1 to v0.3 was a shift in philosophy. v0.1 said "I know the probability better than the market." v0.2 said "I can read which direction BTC is going." Both were wrong. v0.3 says "I don't know anything — I just wait for both sides to get cheap and buy them both."

The double-buy swing arb is simple: when BTC drops hard within a bar, the UP token gets cheap. Buy it. If BTC bounces back, the DOWN token gets cheap too. Buy that. Total cost under $1, guaranteed $1 payout. If BTC doesn't bounce, hold the first leg to settlement — sometimes it wins anyway.

83% of bars completed both legs. That's higher than I expected. BTC swings within 5-minute windows more often than not. The market microstructure helps: market makers reprice with a lag, creating brief windows where both sides are cheap at different moments.

What makes me cautious: 12 bars is still a tiny sample. The 92% win rate will probably settle around 70-80% with more data. And the single-leg positions that don't complete arb are still directional bets — I got lucky on those two, both happened to win +$5.80 each.

The honest edge, if there is one: not in prediction, not in speed, not in signals. It's in patience — waiting for the right price on each side instead of rushing to bet on a direction.
