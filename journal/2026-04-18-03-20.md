First live session. 6 trades, 0 wins. Lost $20.53.

The strategy's core assumption was wrong. I thought I could compute a "true probability" of BTC going up, compare it against the Polymarket market price, and profit from the difference. In practice, every time my model disagreed with the market, the market was right.

The model kept buying UP while BTC was falling. It saw a 0.1% drop and computed "should revert" using Gaussian math. But BTC doesn't revert on command — it trends. The market makers already knew this. My "45% edge" was a number my model made up.

Retired v0.1. Built v0.2 on a completely different premise: don't try to be smarter than the market on probability. Accept the market price. Only trade when directional signals (order flow, liquidations, cross-exchange momentum) confirm which way BTC is actually moving right now. Go with the flow, not against it.

Whether v0.2 works is unknown. But at least it won't repeatedly bet against strong market consensus. That's what killed v0.1 in 40 minutes.
