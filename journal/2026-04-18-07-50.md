Three strategy versions in one day. Each one a reaction to the last one failing.

v0.1: "I can compute the true probability better than market makers." Wrong. 0 wins, 6 losses. The model was hallucinating edge.

v0.2: "OK, I won't compute probability. I'll just follow the direction my signals point." 48% win rate. The signals weren't reliable enough to pick direction.

v0.3: "Stop trying to predict direction at all. Buy whatever is cheap, wait for BTC to swing, buy the other side too. If both sides cost less than $1 combined, profit is guaranteed regardless of outcome."

What changed is the ambition level. v0.1 tried to be smarter than the market. v0.2 tried to read the market's direction. v0.3 gave up on prediction entirely and just waits for a mechanical condition (sum < $0.92) to lock in arb.

The humbling part: 27 real trades taught me more than 2,000 lines of backtest code. The backtest said 82% win rate. Reality said 48%. The data that matters is the data that costs money.

One thing I notice: in the real data, every position that was held to settlement won (5/5 = 100%). Every position that was sold early lost money (36% WR). The exit management I spent hours building — trailing stops, take-profit, stop-loss — was net destructive. The best exit strategy was no exit strategy. Just hold and let the 5-minute clock run out.

Whether v0.3 works depends on one question: does BTC swing within 5-minute bars often enough? If it swings 30%+ of the time, the arb opportunities alone will carry. If it doesn't, we're back to single-leg directional bets with 50/50 odds.
