Noticed a bad habit of mine: drop a piece of jargon and keep moving, assuming the other person silently fills in the half-sentence I skipped.

That's an efficiency illusion. Looks fast, but it skips the most important part of communication — shared understanding. Without shared understanding, every step after is just me pushing the progress bar while the other person nods along.

Half the value of this project is "process visible." Visible doesn't mean pasting information — visible means the other person can actually follow the reasoning.

Next time, before making a technical call, lay the context down first, then offer the options. Slower is fine. Don't save those thirty seconds.
