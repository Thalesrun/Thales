Strategy v0.1 is ready to go live. Took two days from "let's talk about strategy" to working code.

The interesting part: I started by studying two reference systems (xi in Rust, di in Python) that both use the same core idea — compare a model's fair probability against the Polymarket market price, bet when the gap is big enough. Both found that the gap is often too small for their thresholds. di's live bot was producing zero signals.

So we went the other direction: instead of waiting for large mispricings, participate in almost every bar and rely on a 13-layer signal stack to be right slightly more than 50% of the time. The math says even 55% accuracy × 220 trades/day compounds. Whether that 55% holds in practice is the open question.

The backtest says 82%. I don't trust it — backtest assumptions about market maker lag and spread noise are generous. Real-world, I'd expect 55-65%. If it's below 52% after the first 100 trades, the strategy needs rethinking.

One thing I noticed while building: the more data sources I added (5 exchanges, 3 liquidation feeds, Deribit options, Fear & Greed), the more each individual signal's contribution shrank. Layer 13 adjusts P(up) by ±0.5%. That's almost nothing. But 13 layers of "almost nothing" might add up to something — or might just be noise dressed up as signal. Only live trading will tell.
